Value-added tax (VAT) thresholds determine which businesses must collect and remit VAT. Setting these thresholds involves tradeoffs between administrative simplicity and revenue collection. This analysis examines how different threshold levels affect government revenues and business compliance burdens.

Key findings:
- Higher thresholds reduce compliance costs for small businesses
- Lower thresholds capture more revenue but increase administrative burden
- The optimal threshold depends on enforcement capacity and economic structure
- Counterfactual analysis is crucial for understanding policy impacts
